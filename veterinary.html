Veterinary Support

Emergency Helpline (1962 MVU Services)

The most prominent and accessible emergency service for livestock in India is the centralized helpline for Mobile Veterinary Units (MVUs).

Dial 1962

This is the common, pan-India emergency veterinary toll-free number being implemented across states under the central government scheme.

Function

When a farmer dials 1962, the call lands at a centralized call center. The center logs the animal's location and ailment and dispatches the nearest Mobile Veterinary Unit (MVU) to the location.

Services Provided by MVUs

These units handle emergency cases, provide on-the-spot treatment, undertake AI (Artificial Insemination), and carry out necessary vaccinations and deworming.

Goal

To ensure that veterinary services reach the animal's location, rather than requiring the farmer to transport a sick animal over long distances.

Online Doctor Consultation (Tele-Veterinary Services)

Online consultation, or Tele-Veterinary Services, is rapidly expanding, primarily through government initiatives and private apps, to bridge the gap between farmers and qualified veterinarians, especially in remote areas.

e-Gopala App

Launched under the Rashtriya Gokul Mission, this official government app provides farmers with direct access to quality veterinary services, including a facility for online consultations (Tele-Veterinary).

Benefits

It allows farmers to receive advice on animal health, feeding, and breeding directly from experts via phone or video call, often solving minor issues without the need for a physical visit, thus saving time and cost.

Focus

These services are critical for quick diagnosis, prescribing common medications, and confirming whether an on-site visit is necessary.
