Government Schemes & Programs - Livestock

Overview

The following government schemes focus on enhancing livestock productivity, promoting entrepreneurship, and conserving India's indigenous breeds.

The umbrella program for livestock development is the Rashtriya Pashudhan Vikas Yojana (RPVY), integrating key initiatives to enhance productivity, promote entrepreneurship, and improve animal health. Programs listed below fall under RPVY or similar central schemes.

  • Entrepreneurship Development and Employment Generation (EDEG): Credit-linked back-ended subsidies for poultry, sheep, goat, and pig farming ventures.
    Goal: Establish backward and forward linkages and generate employment.
  • National Livestock Mission (NLM): Subsidies for small ruminant breeding farms, feed and fodder development, and technology transfer.
    Subsidy: Up to 50% of project cost for individuals, Farmer Producer Organizations (FPOs), and entrepreneurs.
  • Dairy Entrepreneurship Development Scheme (DEDS): Credit-linked subsidy for small dairy units (2â€“10 animals), milk handling/chilling equipment, and dairy product units.
  • Subsidy Structure: 25% of capital cost for general category, 33.33% for SC/ST/Women.
  • Goal: Upgrade technology and bring structural changes in the unorganized dairy sector.
  • Rashtriya Gokul Mission (RGM): Focused on indigenous cattle development and conservation.
  • Goals:
    • Genetic Upgradation: Breed improvement for milch and draught breeds.
    • Infrastructure: Establish Gokul Grams and National Kamdhenu Breeding Centres.
    • Artificial Insemination (AI): Use high-quality, sex-sorted semen to conserve female calves and indigenous bloodlines.
    • Field Performance Recording (FPR): Maintain records to identify superior germplasm.
  • National Programme for Bovine Breeding (NPBB): Mobile AI Labs and expansion of AI network to provide superior breeding services at farmers' doorsteps.
